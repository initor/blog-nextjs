## Implementation Decisions

### Voice & tone
- Sharp opinion piece — confident, assertive, thesis-driven. Not a memoir, not a tutorial.
- Paul Graham essay style — conversational authority, clear thinking, occasionally personal. Thesis-driven structure.
- "Decades to days" phrase appears explicitly in the text (intro and/or closing) — readers can name the thesis after reading.

### Section depth & balance
- Focused post: 5-8 minute read (~1500-2500 words). Every sentence earns its place. Tight, punchy, no filler.
- Hackathon story is the centerpiece of Era 3 — the "aha moment" (10 skills, zero code, failed priors). Workflow deep-dive supports it, not the other way around.
- Eras 1-2 are brief contextual setup — Claude calibrates exactly how brief based on what the narrative needs.

### Narrative arc
- Closing is forward-looking — what this means going forward, what changes when anyone can build like this. A call to rethink what's possible.
- "Decades to days" used explicitly — appears in text so readers can name the thesis.

### Visualization placement
- Set up then show: describe the concept in prose first, then the visualization appears. Reader knows what they're looking at before interacting.
- Brief captions below each visualization guiding interaction: "Click to expand directories" / "Watch the agents work in parallel".
- After visualizations, keep moving — don't reflect on what was just shown. The viz speaks for itself.

### Claude's Discretion
- Opening approach (punchline, scene, or question — whatever serves the sharp PG-style essay best)
- Era transitions (clean time jumps vs. thematic bridges — whatever keeps momentum)
- Eras 1-2 length (calibrate to what the narrative needs without padding)
- Honesty level about failed prior attempts (Slack bot, chatbot, MCP) — calibrate to what serves the story
- Target audience register (fellow engineers vs. broader tech) — pick based on existing blog tone
- Visualization grouping (together in one section vs. separated by prose) — place where they serve the narrative
- Technical depth in workflow section — pick the right level of specificity for a focused, opinionated piece
